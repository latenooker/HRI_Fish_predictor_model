About This Dashboard

This interactive tool presents predictions from a Bayesian spatial multi-species abundance model fitted to coral reef survey data from the Belize Barrier Reef system.

Data Sources:

  • Healthy Reefs Initiative (HRI) 2023 reef surveys
  • Atlantic & Gulf Rapid Reef Assessment (AGRRA) 2011–2021 baseline
  • Bathymetric predictors from TNC 10m resolution rasters

Response Variables (4):

  • Coral Cover (%) — live stony coral percentage
  • Macroalgae Cover (%) — fleshy macroalgae percentage (indicator of reef degradation)
  • Commercial Fish Biomass — snappers, groupers, jacks, barracuda, grunts (g/100m²)
  • Herbivorous Fish Biomass — parrotfish + surgeonfish (g/100m²)

Model: Spatial factor multi-species GLMM (sfMsAbund from spAbundance R package) with NNGP spatial random effects, exponential covariance, and predictors: depth, depth², slope, aspect (N-S, E-W), and terrain roughness.

Key Finding: The spatial model reduced prediction error for fish biomass by 20–25%, indicating strong spatial autocorrelation in reef fish communities along the Belize Barrier Reef.
